What is BenchHub?

BenchHub is an open benchmarking platform. You bring a dataset (often pulled straight from HuggingFace), turn it into a leaderboard with a clear prediction contract, and let anyone submit their model's predictions to be scored by metrics you choose. Every submission is ranked on the leaderboard and can be opened in a deep comparison view that lines up ground truth and predictions for each sample.

What makes it work end to end is a strict typed contract: every field of data has a kind (image, depth, mask, label, a video sequence, …) that both sides agree on, so the dataset, the benchhub-client, and the metric engine all speak the same language.

The end-to-end flow

A typical lifecycle on BenchHub has four stages:

1
Import a dataset

Pull a HuggingFace repo. Tabular datasets are inferred automatically; file-tree repos (paired files, packed archives, video clips) are mapped field by field. Stored as a lightweight preview.

2
Create a leaderboard

Bind the dataset, choose which fields are inputs vs ground truth, pick a sample subset to materialize at full resolution, and declare the prediction contract.

3
Add metrics

Attach scoring functions (Python) and visualizations. Metrics run per-sample and pool into the leaderboard score; outputs can chain into one another.

4
Submit & compare

Use benchhub-client to pull inputs, run your model, and submit typed predictions. The leaderboard ranks them and the comparison view shows every sample side by side.

Signing in

BenchHub never stores passwords. You can sign in three ways — all create your account on first use:

  • GitHub or Google (OAuth, one click);
  • Email — enter your address and we mail you a 6-digit code to verify it (no password).

All three land you on your home dashboard. See the User Guide for accounts, quotas, and visibility.

What you can do

  • Browse the public catalog of leaderboards and datasets, with per-card stats (datasets, contributors, submissions).
  • Import datasets from HuggingFace — tabular or file-tree, with a draft you never lose.
  • Materialize a chosen sample subset at full resolution per leaderboard, on your storage quota.
  • Author metrics & visualizations in Python, kept in a reusable library with public/private visibility.
  • Submit predictions programmatically and re-score on demand.
  • Compare submissions sample-by-sample, including video sequences, depth colormaps, and confusion matrices.
