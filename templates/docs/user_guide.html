User guide

Accounts, navigation, visibility, and quotas — everything around the core flow.

Signing in

There are no passwords. Pick whichever is convenient — each creates your account on first use and lands you on your home dashboard:

  • Continue with GitHub / Continue with Google — one-click OAuth.
  • Email — type your address; we email a 6-digit code; enter it to verify. Codes expire in 10 minutes and are single-use.

If you sign in with two providers that share one email, they resolve to the same account.

Your home dashboard

Home is your workspace: your leaderboards and datasets in the main column, and a sidebar with the latest public submissions and your own latest submissions (each showing the primary-metric score and rank). Dataset cards show sample counts and how many leaderboards use them; leaderboard cards show the dataset / contributor / submission counts.

Browsing the catalog

  • Leaderboards — the public catalog. Filter by text, tag, or category; sort by recent activity, newest, or most submissions. A green/yellow pill shows whether a leaderboard's samples are explorable.
  • Datasets — BH-imported datasets plus cached HuggingFace datasets, grouped by area/task.
  • Public profiles at /u/<id> show a user's public datasets and leaderboards.

Visibility & publishing

Datasets, leaderboards, metrics, and visualizations each have a visibility:

  • public — in the catalog, visible to all;
  • unlisted — reachable by link, hidden from catalogs;
  • private — only you (and admins).

Things you create default to private. Flip visibility on the object's settings page (e.g. a dataset's /dataset/<id>/settings). Publishing a private item pre-checks your public storage budget first.

Storage quotas

Two independent budgets keep working space and shared content from competing:

  • Public — 100 GB by default, for anything public.
  • Private — 10 GB by default, for private/unlisted content.

Imports are cheap (the small preview tier); the real cost is per-leaderboard materialization, charged to the leaderboard owner. Submission ZIPs aren't charged to you. Your usage is shown on Account settings.

Account & tokens

  • Account settings — profile, quota usage, and the danger zone. Deleting your account requires typing your email to confirm; it removes your datasets, leaderboards, and the metrics and visualizations you authored, and detaches your submissions on other people's leaderboards.
  • API tokens — create the Bearer token the client uses. Regenerating invalidates the old token immediately.

Requesting features & data types

Need a dataset imported or a new capability? File it via Feature requests; admins triage with a status and note.

Ready to build something? Start with Importing data or jump to a tutorial.
