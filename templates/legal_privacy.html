Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-05-06 · Draft — review with counsel before launch.

What we collect

  • Account info from your OAuth provider: name, email, avatar URL, and the provider's user ID. We use this to identify your account.
  • Content you upload: datasets, predictions, metric code, leaderboards, and metadata. Visibility settings on each item determine who else can see them.
  • Operational data: upload timestamps, processing status, quota usage. Used to operate the Service and enforce fair-use limits.
  • Server logs: request paths, status codes, IP addresses, user agents. Retained briefly for debugging and abuse prevention.

How we use it

  • To provide the Service: render leaderboards, run metrics, attribute uploads to your account.
  • To enforce quotas and detect abuse.
  • To send transactional notices about your account or content (when notification features are enabled).

We don't sell your data. We don't run third-party advertising trackers.

What we share

Public-visibility content is, by definition, visible to anyone on the internet — including your display name and avatar attached to leaderboard entries. Private and unlisted content is not surfaced in catalogs or search.

We may share data when legally required (subpoenas, court orders).

Cookies

We use a small number of cookies that are necessary for the Service to function: a session cookie holding your login state, and an active_project_id cookie remembering which project you have selected. We do not set advertising or cross-site tracking cookies.

We also use Google Analytics to understand aggregate traffic (page views, referrers, approximate location and device). Google sets analytics cookies in your browser for this purpose; its handling of this data is governed by the Google Privacy Policy. You can opt out using Google’s opt-out browser add-on.

Your choices

  • You can delete content you uploaded at any time.
  • You can revoke API tokens at any time from your settings.
  • You can request account deletion by contacting the project maintainer.

Security

We take reasonable measures to protect your data, but no system is perfectly secure. API tokens are stored in our database; if you suspect a token has been exposed, regenerate it immediately.

Changes

We may update this Policy. Material changes will be flagged on the landing page or via email when notification features are available.

Questions: open an issue in the repository or contact the project maintainer.
