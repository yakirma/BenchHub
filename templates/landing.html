How it works

Three steps from idea to ranked leaderboard.

1. Pick a dataset

Use your own data or pick one from the public catalog. Images, scalars, depth maps, audio — BenchHub handles the common ML data types.

2. Define a leaderboard

Pick which fields submissions are scored against, assign each a role (input vs ground truth), then attach the metrics and visualisations to run on every submission.

3. Submit, get ranked

Send your predictions, BenchHub scores them, and your run lands on the leaderboard next to everyone else's.

Submit in three lines

Install the client, point it at a leaderboard, send your predictions. BenchHub validates the types, scores every metric, and ranks you automatically.

See supported data types 
# pip install benchhub-client
import benchhub as bh
client = bh.Client(token="<your-token>")
sub = client.submission(lb_id).predict(sample, label="cat").submit()

Sharing & storage

Every dataset and leaderboard is public or private. Generous storage on either side.

Public

50 GB
  • Listed on /datasets and /leaderboards for everyone
  • Anyone can submit predictions and appear on the board
  • Counted against your 50 GB public quota

Private

10 GB
  • Visible only to you — not listed anywhere
  • Iterate quietly; Publish with one click when ready
  • Counted against your 10 GB private quota
